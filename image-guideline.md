# Photograph Guidelines for Fashion Store Website

This document provides expert recommendations for the types of photographs to use on each page and section of the website. Follow these guidelines to ensure a visually appealing, cohesive, and professional web design.

---

## 1. Landing Page (Home)
- **Hero Banner:**
  - Use a high-resolution, professionally shot lifestyle photograph that reflects the brand's essence (e.g., a model in signature attire, boutique interior, or a curated flat lay).
  - Prefer natural lighting, minimal distractions, and a clean background.
  - Image should evoke aspiration, luxury, and approachability.
- **Brand Tagline/Description:**
  - Subtle background image or texture (e.g., soft fabric, boutique ambiance) with low opacity for depth.

## 2. About Section
- **Brand Story/Philosophy:**
  - Portrait of the founder or team in the boutique setting.
  - Candid shots of the boutique, workspace, or creative process.
  - Detail shots of signature products or materials.
- **Highlights:**
  - Collage or grid of milestone moments (e.g., store opening, events, awards).
  - Use images that convey authenticity and craftsmanship.

## 3. Fashion & Collection
- **Collection Cards:**
  - Each collection should have a hero image: model shots, styled product flat lays, or editorial-style photos.
  - Use consistent lighting and background for all collection images for a unified look.
  - Highlight textures, colors, and unique features of each collection.
- **Features/Badges:**
  - Use close-up shots for fabric, patterns, or details.
- **View All Collections CTA:**
  - Optional: A group shot or dynamic image showing multiple products/looks.

## 4. Lifestyle & Gifts
- **Category Cards:**
  - Use aspirational lifestyle images for each category (e.g., beauty: skincare in use, home decor: styled living space, jewelry: close-up on model, stationery: creative workspace, seasonal: festive setups).
  - Images should be bright, inviting, and contextually relevant.
- **Features Section:**
  - Use icons or small supporting images (e.g., brand logos, curated gift boxes).
- **CTA:**
  - Optional: A vibrant, happy customer or gift-giving moment.

## 5. Experience Section
- **Experience Hubs (Peppermint Café, 24seven Store):**
  - High-quality interior and ambiance shots of the café and store.
  - Action shots: people dining, shopping, or engaging with the space.
  - Feature images: signature dishes, best-selling products, or unique experiences.
- **Timeline/Steps:**
  - Use small, thematic icons or illustrations to support each step.

## 6. Contact/Visit Page
- **Location/Map:**
  - Exterior shot of the boutique with clear signage.
  - Map image or embedded Google Maps screenshot (if allowed).
- **Contact Details:**
  - Team or staff portraits (optional, for a personal touch).
- **Visit Highlights:**
  - Photos of in-store experiences, customer interactions, or boutique ambiance.

---

## General Image Guidelines
- **Quality:** Always use high-resolution, professionally edited images (minimum 1920px width for hero/large banners).
- **Orientation:** Prefer landscape for banners/sections, portrait for team or product shots.
- **Consistency:** Maintain a consistent color palette, lighting style, and mood across all images.
- **Branding:** Avoid stock images that do not match the brand’s identity. Use original photography whenever possible.
- **Optimization:** Compress images for web (use modern formats like WebP) without losing quality.
- **Accessibility:** Add descriptive alt text for all images.
- **Legal:** Ensure all images are licensed for commercial use or are original content.

---

> **Tip:** Plan a professional photoshoot covering all the above needs, including wide shots, close-ups, candid moments, and product details. Use natural light and real settings for authenticity.
